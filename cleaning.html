It's time for Cleaning the Tank

First of all, only use water that you have conditioned. If you don't have a water conditioner, you should use water that you drink. Some tap water have metals or rust on it which could kill fishes. Sometimes chlorine is present as well, which is why you are gonna need clean purified water. Distilled works too as well as mineral water.

Using A Siphon

Vacuum out any fish poop or left over food from the aquarium. Once you have vaccumed out the bottom of the tank, you can then proceed to remove 60% of the water. Leave out 40% in order to keep the beneficial bacteria that keeps the fishes alive.

Scrub the Tank

You can scrub the inside of the tank with a sponge. But don't add soap or any chemicals. Just scrub the surface until it is no longer foggy.

Squeeze your Sponge Filter

If you have a sponge filter, pull it out of the tank first then using the water from the tank that you removed, squeeze the sponge in order to remove its contents. DONT use soap to clean the sponge. The Sponge filter acts as a home for the beneficial bacteria that decreases ammonia levels and nitrite. Cleaning the sponge filter with soap kills these beneficial bacteria. Just squeeze the sponge filter and you will be fine.

You are now Ready to Start your Fishkeeping Journey!

You are now equipped with the knowledge to keep fishes in your house! Remember, some fish will die in the process. The best thing we can do is to prevent it from happening in the future. Happy fishkeeping!
